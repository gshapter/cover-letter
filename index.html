Hey, I'm George

Who?

I'm a product designer from London, U.K. I've been designing and launching digital products for over 8 years. Currently I lead product design at Papier, and before that I co-founded a small agency, Qip Creative.

I love making things, and setting myself ambitious personal projects. Currently I spend a lot of my time painting and cooking.

Why Basecamp?

My work has always combined a bunch of roles into one: product strategist, researcher, UX/UI designer, and more. It's a holistic approach to product design that Basecamp shares. I know this because I developed my own ideas in part by reading Rework, Getting Real, and the SvN blog.

In past roles it has always been necessary for me to proactively develop and communicate my own goals – and then execute on them. Often this has meant learning new skills. I've had to be a self-starter, and I'm no stranger to the 'manager of one' concept. This broad operating range is one of my strengths, and something I want to retain as I progress in my career. Basecamp is the best place I can think of to do this.

OK, so you think you're a good fit then?

I could be! I'm a conscientious, creative person with a desire to build extraordinary things. I have years of experience in iterative software development. I enjoy talking to customers and understanding Jobs to be Done. I'm at home when designing and prototyping (pen and paper, Sketch, Figma, Adobe Suite, Framer, in the browser, etc.). Most of all I enjoy working with a small team to ship experiences that customers love. People also really like working with me. You can ask them.

I should be honest about my weakness now: writing production code is not currently part of my job. However, I have built simple websites before, like the one you're on now. I also write code outside of work. This year I have been learning Ruby, for example. Throughout my career I have worked closely with – sitting right next to – Rails developers, so I'm familiar with how Rails apps work, and with .erb.

But I'm not an expert (yet). Front-end development is an area I will need to work on, and I'm excited about the opportunity to do so. I believe the gap between where I am and where you need me to be is easily bridged.

Why now?

I'm leaving the U.K. and moving to the states. My wife is a US citizen and has been in New York for a couple of years for work. Keeping my job wasn't an option (no remote work at Papier!), so I handed in my notice this summer. My last day is December 11th – just in time for me to start with you?

Leaving Papier has been a tough decision. It's a business I helped found, and it's going from strength to strength. The team is amazing. The people I hired when we ran Qip are still working with me. I've been building websites with Papier's CTO for almost a decade. Why am I telling you this? Because Basecamp is a rare example of a technology company that expects it's team to stick around. I'm looking for somewhere to spend the next 10 years.
